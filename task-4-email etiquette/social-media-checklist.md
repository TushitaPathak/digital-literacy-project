# As a responsible social media user, you should: #


# Do’s #

- **Think before posting**

  Pause and ask: “Could this offend, mislead, or hurt someone?” Once it’s online, it can be hard to fully erase.

- **Use strong passwords and 2‑factor authentication**

  Create unique passwords for each account and turn on two‑factor authentication to reduce hacking risk.

- **Set privacy settings carefully**
  
  Make sure only people you trust can see sensitive info; limit who can tag you, message you, or see your posts.

- **Be respectful and kind**
  
  Criticize ideas, not people; avoid insults, sarcasm that can be misread, and public shaming.

- **Fact‑check before sharing**
  
  Verify news, memes, and “viral” posts; avoid spreading misinformation or half‑true claims.

- **Check your “friends” and followers**
  
  Regularly review who follows you or is in your network; remove or block unknown or suspicious accounts.

- **Engage, don’t just scroll**
  
  Reply thoughtfully, support others, and add value to conversations instead of only consuming content.

- **Take breaks and limit screen time**
    
  Schedule social‑media‑free hours to protect your mental health, sleep, and real‑life relationships.



# Don’ts #

- **Don’t overshare personal details**

  Never post your full home address, ID/photo, bank details, OTPs, or exact home‑alone schedules.

- **Avoid sharing when emotions are high**

   what feels funny or “honest” in the moment can haunt you later.

- **Don’t harass, bully, or troll**

  Don’t send abusive messages, body‑shame, or encourage hate against any individual or group.

- **Don’t spam or over‑promote**

  Don’t bombard feeds with repeated ads, invites, or schemes; it damages your credibility and annoys followers.

- **Don’t share others’ private content without consent**

   Never post screenshots of DMs, private photos, or documents without permission, even if they’re “funny.”
