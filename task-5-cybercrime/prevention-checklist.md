 ### Stay Safe Online Checklist for College Students in India

- **Use strong, unique passwords**: \
Create passwords with 12+ characters mixing letters, numbers, and symbols (e.g., "MP2026Badminton!"). Use a password manager app like LastPass or Bitwarden, and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on college email, Google Classroom, and banking apps.\
- **Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks**: \
Skip logging into bank accounts or UPI apps (like Google Pay or PhonePe) on college cafe hotspots. Use mobile data or a trusted VPN like ExpressVPN to encrypt your connection.\
- **Verify links before clicking**: \
Hover over suspicious emails/SMS/WhatsApp links claiming "scholarship alerts" or "fee refunds." If the URL looks off (e.g., fakebank.in), delete it\'97phishing scams target students during exam seasons.\
- **Limit social media oversharing**:\
 Set Instagram/Facebook profiles to private, avoid posting Aadhaar/PAN details or live locations from hostels, and think twice before accepting friend requests from "job recruiters."\
- **Update apps and devices regularly**: \
Enable auto-updates on your Android/iOS phone and laptop (e.g., VS Code extensions). Outdated software is a hacker's easy entry\'97check monthly via Settings > Software Update.\
- **Secure financial transactions**\
Never share UPI PIN or OTPvia calls claiming to be from your bank (e.g., "SBI fraud alert"). Use app biometrics and set transaction limits (\uc0\u8377 5,000/day) in BHIM/Paytm to block big losses.\
 **Double-check beneficiary details**:\
 Before UPI transfers\'97scammers pose as "roommates" or "tutees" on WhatsApp groups. If something feels urgent (e.g., "Pay now for laptop deal"), pause and verify via official app.\
- **Install reputable antivirus**: Use free tools like Avast or Malwarebytes on your phone/laptop. Scan weekly, especially after downloading study PDFs or AI design tools from untrusted sites.\
- **Backup important data**: Regularly save college assignments, design portfolios, and photos to Google Drive or iCloud with 2FA enabled. This protects against ransomware targeting students.\
- **Report incidents immediately**:\
 Use the National Cyber Crime Portal(cybercrime.gov.in) to file complaints online, or call the helpline **1930** for cyber fraud/financial scams. Act within 24 hours for best recovery chances, especially UPI disputes.\
